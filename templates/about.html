About Early Bird

Catching important dates early so you never miss a deadline

Our Story

As students in the Master of Management program at the Haskayne School of Business, we were tired of manually entering every single assignment, exam, and deadline from our syllabi into our calendars.

With Early Bird, users can upload a course outline (or any PDF with date-based content), and the app will automatically extract key dates and generate a downloadable .ics calendar file. The file works with Google Calendar, Outlook, and most other calendar platforms. What used to take 30 minutes now takes just a few seconds.

This app was built as our final project for ENTI 674: Technologies of Innovation, where we explored the real-world applications of generative AI, no-code, and low-code tools. From idea to execution, we used platforms like Replit, Claude, and ChatGPT to guide development, streamline coding, and deliver a product we genuinely wish existed at the start of the program.

The Problem We Solved

At the beginning of each semester, students spend hours manually transferring deadlines from course outlines into their calendars.

Our mission is to eliminate this tedious task, helping students, professors, and academic professionals save time and stay organized by automating the extraction of important dates from documents.

How It Works

Our application uses advanced text analysis and pattern recognition to identify assessment deadlines in academic documents. It's specifically designed to extract:

  • Assignment due dates
  • Quiz and exam schedules
  • Project deadlines
  • Presentation dates

Calendar Integration

Once dates are extracted, they can be seamlessly exported to:

  • Google Calendar
  • Microsoft Outlook
  • Apple Calendar (via .ics files)
  • Any calendar app that supports .ics format

Stay organized across all your devices with automatic synchronization.

Privacy & Security

We take your privacy seriously. The documents you upload are processed temporarily and not permanently stored on our servers. Your information is never shared with third parties.

When you connect your Google or Microsoft account, we only request the minimum necessary permissions to add events to your calendar, and you can revoke these permissions at any time.
